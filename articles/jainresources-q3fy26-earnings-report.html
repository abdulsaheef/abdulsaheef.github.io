TL;DR
Revenue jumped ~30% QoQ in Q3 FY26. Margins compressed due to costs, yet PAT and EPS improved.
Near-term trigger: Monitoring Agency Report (Feb 13, 2026).

What Changed in Q3 FY26

Jain Resource Recycling Ltd (NSE: JAINREC) delivered a strong volume-led quarter. Top-line growth was sharp, but rising finance and employee costs kept margins under pressure.

This quarter is best understood as scale first, efficiency later.

The Margin–Profit Paradox

  • Revenue scale expanded faster than costs
  • Finance costs rose post IPO & JV formation
  • Employee costs increased due to Labour Codes
  • Absolute profit rose despite margin compression

Segment Pulse

  • Aluminium: Volume-led growth
  • Lead: Stable contribution
  • Copper: Volatile but improving mix

Strategic Expansion: Kuwait Acquisition

  • 25% stake in Abraj Al Khaleej, Kuwait
  • Cash deal ≤ USD 3 million
  • Non-ferrous metal & battery recycling

This acquisition acts as a Middle-East platform play, not a one-off investment.

Key Risk & Near-Term Trigger

Monitoring Agency Report adjourned to Feb 13, 2026. This will decide short-term market sentiment.

Final Take

Q3 FY26 confirms growth momentum, not margin strength — yet. The investment case now hinges on execution and transparency.
